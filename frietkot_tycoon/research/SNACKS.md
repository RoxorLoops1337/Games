# Frietkot Tycoon — Fried Snacks & Meats Research

Deep-dive on the *fried snacks and meats* sold at a Belgian **frituur / frietkot** (the stuff
next to the fries). Research reference for menu design, art, copy, and the (satirical)
quality-vs-cost mechanic. Belgian street-food framing, 1990s-onward.

> Vocabulary note: the shop is a **frituur** (Flanders), **friterie / friture / baraque à frites**
> (Wallonia/Brussels), or **frietkot / frietkraam** in slang. Snacks are ordered by the piece and
> almost always paired with a paper cone/tray of *frieten/frites* and a sauce. "**Een grote met
> stoofvlees**" = a *large* fries topped with beef stew — see Stoofvlees below.
> The frituur was recognized as intangible cultural heritage in Flanders, and fries themselves are
> a national identity marker. [Source](https://www.brusselstimes.com/1667605/fries-with-that-how-chips-became-a-staple-of-belgian-identity)

## 1. The Sausage-Family Snacks (the deep-fried "meat sticks")

### Frikandel — the king of the frituur
- **What it is:** A skinless, cylindrical, dark-brown, smooth-textured minced-meat "sausage,"
  deep-fried (not grilled). Chewy, savory, mildly spiced. The single most iconic frituur snack and,
  in the Netherlands, the #1 fast-food snack overall. ~600 million produced per year in NL alone.
  [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frikandel)
- **Ingredients:** A finely-ground blend of **mechanically separated chicken (~40%)** and **pork
  (~25%)**, plus water, breadcrumbs, thickener (rusk/flour), onion, herbs, spices and flavor
  enhancers. Historically some producers added a little **horse meat** (~5%), now mostly removed
  because horse is taboo (and, ironically, too expensive). The smooth paste is what gives it that
  uniform, un-meaty texture. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanically_separated_meat) [Source](https://dutchreview.com/culture/frikandel/)
- **History:** Word derives from the 17th-century *frikadel/frickedil* (a spiced minced-veal
  patty). The **modern** smooth deep-fried frikandel was industrialized in the 1950s: a "frikadel"
  in 1954 and the smoother "frikandel" in 1958, with a different production method. Spread through
  Dutch and Belgian frituurs from the '50s onward. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frikandel) [Source](https://horecawebzine.com/frituur-frikandel-curryworst-lange-hamburger/)
- **How cooked/eaten:** Deep-fried ~3 min from frozen. Rarely eaten dry — always with sauce.
- **Region:** Universal across Belgium and the Netherlands.
- **Price tier:** Cheap. ~€2.60 plain; ~€3.00 speciaal; XXL ~€4.50. [Source](https://www.frituur-eddy.be/menu-frituur/)

### Frikandel speciaal
- The frikandel **sliced open lengthwise** and filled/topped with **mayonnaise, curry ketchup, and
  chopped raw onion**. The default "upgrade" order and a rite of passage. [Source](https://horecawebzine.com/frituur-frikandel-curryworst-lange-hamburger/) [Source](https://dutchreview.com/culture/frikandel/)

### Curryworst / Curry(worst) speciaal — and "lange hamburger"
- A **spicier, coarser, firmer-bite** relative of the frikandel, eaten with curry sauce; the
  *speciaal* adds onions + sauces like the frikandel. Fascinating regional naming: around
  **Antwerp** and Flemish Brabant the same sausage is literally *called* a "curryworst"; in
  **Belgian Limburg** it's a **"lange hamburger"** (long hamburger). Great regional-flavor detail
  for a map/region mechanic. [Source](https://horecawebzine.com/frituur-frikandel-curryworst-lange-hamburger/)

### Viandel
- A **crispier cousin of the frikandel**: same soft seasoned-meat interior but wrapped in a
  **crunchy fried jacket** and fried longer, so it's firmer with a crackly shell. Name comes from
  the town of **Vianen**; Mora's "Viandel" is the best-known brand. [Source](https://frietfestijn.nl/smakelijkheden/snacks/)

### Mexicano
- **Flattened, ridged, rectangular** spicy minced-meat snack that (if you squint) resembles a rack
  of ribs. Made from **beef, chicken and pork** with chili/paprika seasoning. Despite the name it's
  **Dutch**, launched by producer De Vries to celebrate the qualifiers for the **1986 Mexico World
  Cup**. Eaten with mayo, curry ketchup, peanut or chili sauce. Clones exist under names like
  *Carrero, Chileno, Belcanto, Ribburger*. [Source](https://www.thebelgianfry.com/product/mexicano/) [Source](https://www.toineskitchen.com/recipes/mexicano/)

### Cervela / Cervelas (the butterflied "gekruld")
- A pre-cooked **pork sausage in a natural casing** that is a genuine Belgian frituur icon. The
  signature move: it is **scored/slashed before frying so it curls open ("gekruld"/butterfly)** into
  a crispy, splayed shape — the cuts spring apart in the hot fat. Served plain, or **"cervela
  speciaal"** with sauce; often eaten with **curry ketchup**. Related to the Swiss/French
  *cervelat*. [Source](https://spoor1.store/products/cervela) [Source](https://degrotegoesting.be/product/cervela-speciale/)
- **Price tier:** ~€3.00–€3.50. [Source](https://www.frituur-r6.be/prijslijst)

## 2. The Burger Family

### Bicky Burger — the cult Belgian burger
- **What it is:** THE Belgian fast-food burger and a genuine cultural icon — a **sesame-seed bun**
  with a **deep-fried patty**, pickles, fried onions and the three branded sauces. Loud, junky,
  beloved. [Source](https://www.tasteatlas.com/bicky-burger)
- **Patty:** A deep-fried patty classically made from a blend of **pork, chicken and horse meat**
  (the horse content is part of its mythology/notoriety). [Source](https://www.tasteatlas.com/bicky-burger)
- **The three mythical Bicky sauces** (this is the whole personality of the burger):
  - **Bicky Dressing** — creamy, tangy, pickled-note dressing made with cucumber/gherkin, onion,
    cabbage, cauliflower and mustard (a fine relish-mayo).
  - **Bicky Tomato / Bicky Ketchup** — a slightly sweet tomato sauce.
  - **Bicky Hot** — the spicy one.
  A "real" Bicky uses all three. [Source](https://www.saucer.fr/bicky-burger-le-burger-culte-belge-et-ses-3-sauces-mythiques/) [Source](https://www.tasteatlas.com/bicky-burger)
- **History:** Created by the **Dutch company Beckers in 1981**; it took off in Belgium far more
  than in NL and became a symbol of eat-what-you-want frituur culture. Fully mainstream by the '90s.
  [Source](https://www.tasteatlas.com/bicky-burger)
- **Variants:** Bicky XXL, Bicky Chicken, Bicky Veggie exist, but the classic is the one.
- **Price tier:** Mid. Roughly €3.50–€5.
- **Trivia hook:** The horse-meat + mystery-sauce combo makes the Bicky a perfect comedic
  "premium junk" tier for the game.

### Hamburger / Vleesburger
- The plain frituur hamburger: a simple fried beef/pork patty on a bun with ketchup/mayo. Baseline
  burger option; the Bicky is the flashy upsell. [Source](https://www.frituur-eddy.be/menu-frituur/)

### Mitraillette ("submachine gun") — the loaded sandwich
- **Half a baguette** loaded with **fried meat (frikandel, burger, cervela, or steak) + a scoop of
  fries + sauce** (andalouse, garlic, mayo, samurai...). Optionally crudités/cheese. Originated in
  **Brussels**, popular with students across Belgium and French Flanders (where it's also called an
  *"Américain"*). The ultimate hangover/late-night order and a natural "combo meal" in-game.
  [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitraillette) [Source](https://www.saveur.com/brussels-sandwich-mitraillette/)

## 3. Balls, Skewers & Chicken

### Boulet / Vleesballetje (meatball)
- A big deep-fried **meatball of pork + beef**, especially loved in **Wallonia** (French-speaking
  Belgium), where "boulet" is the standard word. Can be ordered as **boulet speciaal** with sauce
  and onions. In Liège the sit-down cousin is *boulets à la liégeoise* (meatballs in sweet-sour
  syrup sauce), but at the frituur it's the fried snack. [Source](https://ohmydish.com/recipe/belgian-meatballs)

### Berenpoot / Berenhap / Berenklauw ("bear's paw/bite")
- A **sliced meatball + fried onion rings threaded on a wooden skewer, deep-fried, and drowned in
  peanut (saté) sauce.** More a Dutch/border snack but shows up in Belgian frituurs. Many regional
  names: *berenhap, berenklauw, berenpoot, gehaktbal speciaal,* and **"spoetnik"** (Sputnik) in
  Limburg, where it's eaten with curry sauce instead of peanut. Sometimes pineapple or bell pepper
  join the onion. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berenhap)

### Saté / Brochette
- **Saté:** seasoned chicken (or pork) chunks on a skewer, deep-fried, served with peanut sauce.
- **Brochette:** breaded meatball pieces (pork + poultry) **alternating with onion** on a skewer.
  Both are the "on-a-stick" tier. [Source](https://frietfestijn.nl/smakelijkheden/snacks/)

### Kipcorn
- A **breaded, spiral/corn-cob-shaped fried chicken snack** — crispy shell, chicken inside. Fry
  from frozen ~5 min at 175°C. Kid-friendly. [Source](https://bitterballen.net/recepten/snacks/frituren/)

### Kipnuggets / Chicken bites
- Breaded, deep-fried chicken-fillet pieces. The safe, kid-and-tourist default. [Source](https://frietfestijn.nl/smakelijkheden/snacks/)

### Cordon bleu
- A flat, crêpe/schnitzel-like breaded product **filled with chicken and ham** (sometimes cheese),
  deep-fried. Bigger, "meal-sized" snack. [Source](https://frietfestijn.nl/smakelijkheden/snacks/)

### Ribbetjes (spare ribs) & Sitostick-adjacent items
- **Ribbetjes:** small marinated pork ribs offered at some frituurs (often as a portion/meal rather
  than a single snack). A heartier, pricier option. [Source](https://degrotegoesting.be/product-categorie/snacks/)

## 4. Croquettes (kroketten) — the breaded, creamy-ragout family

Croquettes are a **thick binder (béchamel/roux or brown ragout) + filling, breaded and deep-fried**,
crispy shell / molten creamy heart. The form was invented by French chef **François Massialot in
1691** for Louis XIV's court and became a Belgian staple. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croquette)

### Kaaskroket (cheese croquette)
- Creamy cheese béchamel in a crispy crumb; the vegetarian-friendly crowd favorite. Must be
  "crispy outside, molten cheesy inside." ~€2.40–€3.90. [Source](https://www.informations.brussels/kaaskroketten/)

### Garnaalkroket (grey shrimp croquette) — the "posh" one
- The **premium** croquette: a creamy ragout studded with **North Sea grey shrimp** (good ones are
  ~30% shrimp), tied to the **Belgian coast** and its fishing culture. The classiest, priciest
  frituur snack — a natural top-tier unlock. ~€3.60–€5.00. [Source](https://www.vangeloven.com/producten/de-bourgondier/garnaalkroket-12x7000g) [Source](https://www.frituur-r6.be/prijslijst)

### Ragoutkroket / Vleeskroket & Vol-au-vent kroket
- **Vleeskroket/ragoutkroket:** classic meat-ragout croquette (the Dutch snackbar default).
- **Vol-au-vent kroket:** a **chicken + mushroom ragout** croquette — a handheld spin on the
  sit-down vol-au-vent (see below). [Source](https://www.amuuzbouche.com/products/vol-au-vent)

### Bitterballen
- **Round mini-croquettes** of beef/veal ragout, breaded and fried; classic **borrelhapje** (bar
  snack) eaten by the handful with mustard. More a café/bar snack than a frites order, but on many
  frituur menus. [Source](https://www.bitterballen.net/)

## 5. Spring-Roll Family

### Sitostick — the Belgian frituur original
- **Cubes of turkey meat alternating with white onion, dipped in batter and deep-fried** into a
  crunchy, croquette-like stick — *not* a paper-wrapped spring roll despite being loempia-adjacent.
  **Invented in the 1970s by Jan Mertens of Mol-Millegem (Belgium)**; the "Sitostick" name later
  became a **Mora** brand after Ad van Geloven bought the company. Eaten with mayo or curry.
  Distinctly Belgian. [Source](https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sitostick) [Source](https://degrotegoesting.be/product/sitostick/)

### Loempia (Vietnamese-Belgian spring roll)
- The frituur **loempia** is a large, crispy, golden **pastry-wrapped spring roll** filled with
  minced meat, bean sprouts and cabbage. Introduced to the Low Countries via Indonesian/Chinese
  and, importantly, **Vietnamese immigrants who arrived in the 1970s–80s** and opened restaurants —
  the "Vietnamese loempia" became a beloved Dutch/Belgian snack, distinct from the batter-crust
  sitostick. [Source](https://www.scmp.com/magazines/post-magazine/food-drink/article/2115042/vietnamese-loempia-spring-rolls-long-journey-jin) [Source](https://www.vietnam.vn/en/mon-gio-cha-vietnamese-loempia-duoc-nguoi-dan-ha-lan-cuc-yeu-thich/)

## 6. The Stews & Warm Dishes (topped on fries)

### Stoofvlees / Carbonade Flamande / Stoverij — the big one
- **Flemish beef-and-beer stew:** chunks of beef braised low-and-slow in **dark Belgian beer**
  (Oud Bruin / brown abbey / Flanders Red) with **mustard, bay, thyme, clove**, thickened with a
  slice of mustard-smeared bread; East/West Flanders often add **prunes**. It's the undisputed #1
  classic Flemish dish — TV chef Jeroen Meus even declared **March 1st "National Beef Stew & Fries
  Day."** [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flemish_stew) [Source](https://carryitlikeharry.com/belgiums-winter-survival-food-the-flemish-beef-stew-also-known-as-stoofvlees-and-carbonade-flamande/)
- **At the frituur:** ladled hot over fries as **"stoofvlees met frietjes."** The famous order
  **"een grote met stoofvlees"** = a *large* fries drowned in a scoop of the stew's rich gravy +
  meat. A signature comfort/premium warm item — slow to prep, high satisfaction. Names: *stoofvlees*
  or *stoverij* (Dutch), *carbonade/carbonnade flamande* (French). [Source](https://infernalitch.com/2021/12/22/stoofvlees-met-frieten/)

### Vol-au-vent (koninginnenhap)
- A **puff-pastry shell filled with creamy chicken-and-mushroom ragout** (often with little chicken
  meatballs), served with fries. A beloved sit-down/takeaway Belgian classic; also sold as the
  handheld **vol-au-vent croquette** at frituurs. [Source](https://dagelijksekost.vrt.be/gerechten/vol-au-vent)

## 7. Regional / obscure / 90s-era & modern favorites

- **Pascalleke** — a modern viral "loaded fries" specialty (fries + mayo, curry ketchup, fresh
  onion, a *Belcrunch* crunchy chicken snack, saté spices & fried onions). Invented by **Caroline
  Taghon in Ghent**, named after her father **Pascal** of the famous frituur *De Gouden Saté*; now
  in 60+ frituurs. Part of a wave of trademarked "signature fries" (*Julientje/Julienke,
  Romboutje*). Great model for the game's late-game "signature dish" designer. [Source](https://tastytrails.nl/nieuws/eten/typische-belgische-frituursnacks-van-bickyburger-tot-pascalleke/)
- **Lange hamburger** (Limburg) / **curryworst** (Antwerp) — same sausage, three names (see above).
  Rich material for a region-flavor / localization gag.
- **Spoetnik** (Limburg name for berenhap, eaten with curry not peanut).
- **Cervela speciaal, frikandel speciaal, boulet speciaal** — "speciaal" (= split + sauce + onions)
  is a universal *modifier* across snacks, not one item. Good as an upgrade toggle in-game.
- **Kroketten variety:** kip-, kaas-, garnaal-, groenten-, ragoutkroket — a whole croquette
  sub-family to unlock.
- **Sauces are half the culture:** mayo (the default), curry ketchup, andalouse, samurai (spicy),
  américaine, tartaar, joppiesaus, saté/pinda, ketchup, mammoet, cocktail. Sauce selection is a
  legit gameplay/upsell axis. [Source](https://www.frituur.net/)

## 8. Food-safety / "mystery meat" framing (for the shady quality-vs-cost mechanic)

This is the comedic heart of the "use cheap/rat meat" mechanic — and it's rooted in *real* frituur
lore, so lean satirical, not slanderous:

- **Mystery-meat reputation:** Ask a Belgian/Dutch person what's *in* a frikandel and the running
  joke answers are *"Nobody knows," "Don't ask," "You don't want to know."* This is a real cultural
  meme, perfect flavor text. [Source](https://dutchreview.com/culture/frikandel/)
- **Mechanically separated meat ("separatorvlees"):** the paste of meat scraps pressed off carcass
  bones under high pressure — the base of cheap frikandellen/frikandel-type snacks. Consumer group
  **Foodwatch** has publicly challenged its oversight/traceability (arguing bacteria risk), while
  Dutch authorities maintain it is controlled and documented. Real, ongoing debate. [Source](https://kro-ncrv.nl/programmas/keuringsdienst-van-waarde/wat-is-separatorvlees-en-wat-doet-het-in-de-frikandel) [Source](https://www.frituurwereld.nl/foodwatch-versus-de-frikandel-kort-geding-over-separatorvlees-opgeschort)
- **Historical horror-trivia:** A **1978 investigation** into frikandel contents found chicken
  skins, salivary glands, and cow/pig gastrointestinal tracts in some products — the origin of the
  mystery-meat jokes. Modern regulated products are far tamer. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanically_separated_meat)
- **Horse meat:** genuinely used historically in frikandel and still classically in the **Bicky
  patty** — mostly phased out of frikandellen (taboo + cost). The horsemeat angle is factual, not
  invented, so a "horse content %" dial is fair game. [Source](https://dutchreview.com/culture/frikandel/) [Source](https://www.tasteatlas.com/bicky-burger)
- **Sustainability twist (for a redemption/joke arc):** because MSM uses the *whole* carcass, some
  argue the frikandel is actually one of the **most sustainable/low-waste** snacks around — a fun
  "greenwashing" counter-narrative for the shady-owner mechanic. [Source](https://www.vice.com/nl/article/de-frikandel-is-eigenlijk-de-meest-duurzame-snack/)

**Design framing:** keep it satire-of-industrial-food, not "this shop poisons people." The tradeoff
axis is *cheap filler / mystery MSM / horse / literal rat* → higher margin, lower customer
happiness, rising **health-inspection risk** and reputation hits. Premium tier = real North Sea
shrimp, real beef stew, named-brand sausages.

### Suggested unlock order (progression spine)
1. **Starter kit:** Frieten, mayo/ketchup, **Frikandel**, **Kipnuggets**, **Hamburger**.
2. **Frituur regular:** speciaal upgrades, **Cervela**, **Curryworst**, **Kaaskroket**,
   **Sitostick/Loempia**, sauce rack (andalouse/samurai/curry).
3. **Crowd-pleaser:** **Bicky Burger** (+3-sauce system), **Mexicano**, **Viandel**, **Boulet**,
   skewers (saté/berenpoot), **kipcorn**.
4. **Warm kitchen:** **Stoofvlees** pot (slow-cook station), **Vol-au-vent**, **Mitraillette**
   assembly station, ragout/vleeskroket.
5. **Prestige:** **Garnaalkroket** (coast supplier chain), **signature-fries designer**
   (Pascalleke-style trademarked creations), regional-name reskins for tourism buffs.

### Quality-tier / shady-meat mechanic ideas
- **Per-item meat grade slider:** `Rat/filler → Mystery MSM → Standard MSM (chicken+pork) →
  Horse-blend → Butcher-grade`. Cheaper grade = higher margin + faster spoilage + lower
  satisfaction + rising **inspection risk** and review bombs.
- **Mystery-meat flavor text** on cheap tiers ("Nobody knows what's in it. Don't ask.") for comedy;
  a hidden **"health inspector"** event that scales with how long you've run filler.
- **Reputation vs. margin curve:** filler frikandellen print money early but cap your star rating;
  premium (real North Sea shrimp, real beef stoofvlees, brand-name sausage) unlocks high-end
  clientele, food-critic events, and tourism.
- **"Sustainability spin" perk:** an unlockable PR upgrade that reframes your MSM as
  "zero-waste/whole-carcass eco-snacking" to partially offset the reputation hit — satirical
  greenwashing tie-in.
- **Sauce upsell loop:** every snack can be "speciaal" (split + sauce + onion) for +margin; premium
  branded sauces (Bicky trio, Pascalleke saté-crunch) as purchasable perks.
- **Regional demand:** Wallonia loves boulet; Antwerp orders "curryworst," Limburg "lange
  hamburger"/"spoetnik"; the coast expects garnaalkroket — localize menu naming per map region for
  bonus satisfaction.
