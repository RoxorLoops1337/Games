# The Sauces of the Belgian Frietkot

*Research dossier for **Frietkot Tycoon** — a food-culture deep dive into the single
most important decision a Belgian makes at the fry stand: "Met welke saus?"
("With which sauce?")*

---

## Why sauce is the whole game

In Belgium the fries are sacred, but the **sauce is where the money and the identity
live**. A frituur / friterie (Dutch: *frietkot* / *frituur*; French: *friterie* /
*fritkot*) is judged as much by the length and quality of its sauce list as by its
double-fried potatoes. Even a tiny roadside *frietkot* will offer well over a dozen
cold sauces from a chilled display, plus at least one hot gravy. The heritage file that
Belgium's fry-shop culture used in its bid for official cultural-heritage status lists
the standard range as: mayonnaise, pickles, tartare, cocktail, pepper sauce, andalouse,
bicky, brazil, gypsy (zigeuner), curry ketchup, tomato ketchup, riche, hannibal,
joppie, tomagrec, mystery, américain, frikandelsaus, samurai, mammoet, curry, and
looksaus — and that's the *baseline* ([Immaterieel Erfgoed – Belgische Frietkotcultuur, PDF](https://immaterieelerfgoed.be/files/attachments/.3894/BeschrijvingHistoriek2013_BelgischeFrietkotcultuur.pdf)).

Sauce is also a **profit engine**: cold sauces are cheap, shelf-stable, industrially
produced, and sold at a fat per-portion markup on top of the fries. This is the beating
heart of a tycoon game.

### The sauce map (how the family tree works)

Nearly every cold *frietkot* sauce is a **mayonnaise derivative** — a mayo (or a
mayo-adjacent emulsion) with something stirred in:

- **+ tomato & peppers** → andalouse, américain, brazil, cocktail, mammoet
- **+ chili heat** → samurai, pili-pili
- **+ curry/onion** → joppie, curry (Flemish béchamel style)
- **+ pickled bits / herbs** → tartare, riche, ravigote
- **+ garlic** → looksaus (garlic sauce)

The two big **non-mayo** families are the **tomato line** (tomato ketchup → curry
ketchup) and the **hot gravy line** (stoofvlees / carbonade sauce, pepper sauce).

## The foundation: Belgian mayonnaise (*frietmayonaise*)

Mayonnaise is the undisputed king. Polls put it first almost everywhere: a Takeaway.com
study found **52%** of Belgians name mayo their first choice; a Belviva producer poll
said **55%**; and Belgium's national fry-cooks' union estimates mayo is chosen roughly
**40%** of the time at the counter, versus about **9%** for ketchup ([Brussels Times – "A saucy mystery"](https://www.brusselstimes.com/384182/a-saucy-mystery-how-do-belgians-like-their-fries)).

**What it is / ingredients:** an emulsion of vegetable oil, egg yolk, vinegar (or lemon),
mustard, salt. Belgian *frietmayonaise* is deliberately **richer, thicker and more
stable** than Anglo-American mayo — dense enough to stand up on a fry rather than run
off.

**Colour & taste:** pale ivory to soft cream; velvety, full-fat, mildly tangy, a little
mustardy. Not sweet.

**Why it's different from US mayo — the law:** on **12 April 1955** the King of Belgium
signed a *Royal Decree* on the sale of mayonnaise: to be sold as "mayonnaise" in Belgium
a sauce had to contain **≥ 80% fat (edible vegetable oil)** and **≥ 7.5% egg yolk**.
Those thresholds were far above the rest of Europe (many countries allow ~70–75% oil and
~5% yolk), which is exactly why Belgian mayo tastes eggier and heavier. Mass-market
American mayo often cuts the expensive yolk with water and thickeners, giving a lighter,
whipped, airier texture ([Belgian Smaak – The Long Reign of Belgian Mayonnaise](https://www.belgiansmaak.com/belgian-mayonnaise/); [Focus on Belgium – Creamy mayonnaise on Belgian fries](https://focusonbelgium.be/en/lifestyle/creamy-mayonnaise-belgian-fries)).

**The loophole & the 2016 rewrite:** the 1955 decree could only be enforced against
Belgian producers. Under EU free-movement / mutual-recognition rules, Dutch and Spanish
makers could legally sell their thinner 70%-oil/5%-yolk sauce *in Belgium* as
"mayonnaise," undercutting Belgian firms on their home turf. After industry lobbying, a
new Royal Decree signed **26 May 2016** lowered the baseline to **≥ 70% fat and ≥ 5%
egg yolk** — but created a protected upper tier: only sauce meeting the old **80% fat /
7.5% yolk** standard may be labelled **"traditional" mayonnaise** ([lexgo – Review of Belgian food legislation](https://www.lexgo.be/en/news-and-articles/3792-review-of-belgian-food-legislation-a-first-royal-decree-adopted); [VRT NWS – Belgian government working on new "mayonnaise law"](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/en/2015/07/14/belgian_governmentworkingonnewmayonnaiselaw-1-2390824/); [Flanders Today – Sauce of tension](https://www.flanderstoday.eu/business/sauce-tension-food-industry-wants-rethink-mayonnaise-law)).

**Iconic status:** maximum. Mayo *is* Belgian fries to the outside world, and the
default at every counter. Fun bit of trivia the tourists get wrong: the "French fry with
mayo" cliché is really Belgian.

## The tomato-and-pepper family

### Sauce andalouse — Belgium's beloved

**What it is:** mayonnaise blended with tomato paste and (sweet, roasted) peppers —
pimientos / bell peppers — plus spices. Mildly sweet, gently warm, tangy. Some recipes
lean on caramel for colour ([nl.wikipedia – Andalousesaus](https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andalousesaus)).

**Colour & taste:** salmon-orange to coral pink; creamy, sweet-savoury, faintly spiced,
only barely spicy. Approachable — the "gateway" specialty sauce.

**Origin / history:** despite the Spanish name, it is a **Belgian invention** of the
mid-20th century, not an Andalusian dish. The name borrows the classic French culinary
tag *"à l'andalouse"* (peppers + tomato, "in the Andalusian style"). Two origin claims
compete: the sauce brand **La William** says it created andalouse ~40 years ago, loosely
inspired by gazpacho; rival **Anda Premium Sauces** credits founder Léa
De Boeck-Vandenwyngaert with inventing it in the late 1960s. It spread through friteries
in the 1970s and became a national staple ([Chowhound – How Sauce Andalouse Became The Go-To Dip](https://www.chowhound.com/1718515/dipping-sauce-served-belgian-fries/); [en.wikipedia – Sauce andalouse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sauce_andalouse); [La William – About](https://lawilliam.be/en/about-la-william/)).

**Popularity:** enormous, and **the** regional dividing line. In one poll andalouse was
the **#1 sauce in Wallonia** (French-speaking south) and around **37%** there, while in
Dutch-speaking Flanders it didn't even crack the top three. Nationally it ranks roughly
third overall (~20%), just behind carbonnade ([Brussels Times – "A saucy mystery"](https://www.brusselstimes.com/384182/a-saucy-mystery-how-do-belgians-like-their-fries)).

### Sauce américain (Belgian meaning — careful!)

**What it is:** a **spicy tomato-based** sauce with a herby/spiced garnish — onions,
tomato, a touch of white wine and cognac, cayenne, butter in classic renderings. In the
frituur it reads as a tangy, peppery tomato sauce ([Brusselslife – Welke saus kies je met je friet?](https://www.brusselslife.be/nl/article/welke-saus-kies-je-met-je-friet)).

**Naming trap for the game:** in Belgium **"américain" / "filet américain"** usually
means **raw seasoned steak tartare**, not a fry sauce. As a *sauce*, "américain" is the
tomato-cognac one above — do not confuse it with US-style "fry sauce." The name is said
to come from a French, not American, origin.

**Colour/taste:** red-orange; tangy, lightly boozy, mild heat. Niche but classic.

### Brazil / Brasil (the pineapple one!)

**What it is:** a sweet, exotic tomato sauce with **pineapple** and a "secret" spice mix.
Real commercial recipes (e.g. D&L / La William) list rapeseed oil, sugar, tomato purée,
**pineapple purée (~7%)**, egg yolk, vinegar, mustard, **whisky**, curry, garlic,
spices, colour from carotenes ([Anda Sauces – Brazil](https://andasauces.be/en/product/brazil/); [La William – Brasil](https://lawilliam.be/en/saus/brasil-3/)).

**Colour & taste:** warm amber/orange; distinctly **sweet, fruity-tropical**, curry-
tinged. The love-it-or-hate-it sweet option; a cult favourite.

### Cocktailsaus (cocktail sauce)

**What it is:** the simplest derivative — **mayo + ketchup**, often plus tomato purée,
Worcestershire, a splash of **whisky or cognac**, Tabasco, salt, pepper. Soft, creamy,
pink ([Brusselslife – Welke saus](https://www.brusselslife.be/nl/article/welke-saus-kies-je-met-je-friet)).

**Colour/taste:** pale pink; smooth, mildly sweet-tangy, very mellow. A safe crowd-
pleaser and dip-for-shrimp classic.

### Mammoetsaus (mammoth sauce) — the giant joke

**What it is:** a sweet-and-spicy mayo-based sauce with **tomato purée, garlic and
onion**, often sugar, yellow curry and soy sauce. Named after the **"mammoet"** snack —
an extra-long frikandel — so ordering a "mammoet met mammoetsaus" is a bit of a
frituur in-joke about **going big** ([nl.wikipedia – Mammoetsaus](https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mammoetsaus)).

**Colour/taste:** orange-brown; sweet, garlicky, oniony, gently spiced. Regional
favourite (strong in the Kempen/Antwerp area). The name is pure "supersize" comedy — a
gift for a tycoon-game gag about giant portions.

## The spicy family

### Sauce samurai — the hot one

**What it is:** essentially a **fiery andalouse** — mayonnaise + a tomato element
(ketchup/tomato paste) + **chili paste (harissa or sambal)**, often with extra garlic,
smoked paprika, curry or lime. It is reliably the **hottest** of the standard set ([en.wikipedia – Samurai sauce](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samurai_sauce); [Chili Pepper Madness – Samurai Sauce](https://www.chilipeppermadness.com/recipes/samurai-sauce/)).

**Colour & taste:** deeper red-orange than andalouse; creamy but with a real chili kick,
"warm, fruity" heat from harissa.

**Origin:** a **Belgian creation of the late 20th century** (post-1970s friterie boom),
riding the same wave as andalouse. The "Samurai" name is pure marketing exotica —
fierce, Asian-sounding — with **nothing Japanese in it**.

**Popularity:** the default pick for people who want heat; iconic enough that "samurai"
is a recognised heat level, not just a sauce.

### Pili-pili

**What it is:** a chili/hot sauce named after the African *piri-piri* / bird's-eye
pepper (and, in some frituren, scotch-bonnet-grade heat, 100k–350k Scoville territory).
In the frituur it's the **straight-up hot** option, sometimes a red chili emulsion,
sometimes a spicier mayo variant. Reflects Belgium's Congolese/African culinary threads.

**Colour/taste:** red; sharply spicy, the "if you dare" sauce. Niche, for heat-seekers.

## The curry & onion family

### Joppiesaus (the Dutch import)

**What it is:** a **mild curry-onion mayonnaise** — vegetable-oil mayo with onion and
curry spice, plus a proprietary "secret blend." ([en.wikipedia – Joppiesaus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joppiesaus)).

**Colour & taste:** vivid **turmeric yellow**; mild, sweet-savoury curried mayo, oniony.
Comforting and moreish.

**Origin / history:** invented **circa 2000** by **Janyne "Joppie" de Jager** at Annie's
Snackbar in Glanerbrug, a Dutch village on the German border. Industrial production began
**2002** via **Elite Salades & Snacks** (Neede, NL), who own the trademarked name
*Joppiesaus*. A Dutch export that crossed into Belgium and stuck — notably ranking as a
**top-three sauce in Flanders** in at least one survey ([Brussels Times – "A saucy mystery"](https://www.brusselstimes.com/384182/a-saucy-mystery-how-do-belgians-like-their-fries); [Grokipedia – Joppie sauce](https://grokipedia.com/page/joppie_sauce)).

**Trademark note:** "Joppiesaus" is a protected name; other makers must sell look-alikes
under different names ("Joppy," "curry-onion sauce," etc.).

### Curry (Flemish sense) vs. curry ketchup — an important distinction

This trips people up, and it's worth getting right for the game:

- **Curry ketchup** (Flanders & Germany: *curryketchup*): a **tomato-based** sauce — a
  spiced-up ketchup with a curry-spice blend. Red, sweet-tangy, lightly spiced. The
  German *Currywurst* cousin ([nl.wikipedia – Curry (saus)](https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curry_(saus))).
- **"Curry"** in Flanders can instead mean a **cream / béchamel-based** curry sauce
  (pale, mild, curried). So "curry" and "curry ketchup" are *different animals* depending
  on region. Always clarify at the counter.

**Popularity:** curry ketchup is a solid everyday pick, especially with snacks
(frikandel, kroket) more than with plain fries.

## Tomato ketchup

**What it is:** ordinary tomato ketchup — tomato, vinegar, sugar, spices. Red, sweet-
sour.

**Status in Belgium:** present everywhere but faintly **looked down on** as the
"children's / tourist" choice. Chosen only ~9% of the time versus mayo's ~40% ([Brussels Times – "A saucy mystery"](https://www.brusselstimes.com/384182/a-saucy-mystery-how-do-belgians-like-their-fries)). A purist ordering ketchup on good Belgian fries risks a raised eyebrow.

## The pickled / herby family

### Tartaar / sauce tartare

**What it is:** a **mayonnaise base with finely chopped pickles/gherkins, capers, onion
and herbs** (chervil, chives, parsley) — the fresh, tangy, chunky option. A French
classic naturalised into the frituur ([DeVlaendere / Remia – Tartaresaus](https://www.remiaprofessioneel.nl/producten/koude-sauzen/tartaarsaus/devlaendere-tartaresaus-bokaal-3-l)).

**Naming trap (again):** "tartaar" can also mean **raw minced beef** (steak tartare) in
Dutch — but as a *fry sauce* it's the pickle-herb mayo. **Colour/taste:** off-white with
green/pale flecks; tangy, herby, crunchy.

### Riche (sauce riche)

**What it is:** a **richer, spiced tartare** — tartare-style mayo base lifted with a
special spice blend, sometimes **cranberry**, onion and green herbs; commercial recipes
list rapeseed oil, egg yolk, mustard, vinegar, onion, chervil, parsley, even **anchovy**
([La William – Riche](https://lawilliam.be/en/saus/riche-3/); [Anda Sauces – Riche](https://andasauces.be/en/product/riche/)).

**Colour/taste:** creamy pink-cream; tangy, herbaceous, a touch fancier than tartare —
hence "riche" (rich).

---

## Hannibal

**What it is:** a **Belgian specialty** sauce — slightly sweet, with **crispy fried
onion pieces** through it. Commercial recipes: rapeseed oil, glucose-fructose syrup,
vinegar, ~3% onion + roasted onion, egg yolk, mustard, tomato purée, candy syrup, soy
sauce, spices ([Vandemoortele – Hannibal](https://vandemoortele.com/nl-be/products/sauzen/koude-sauzen/specialiteiten/hannibal-1l); [DeVlaendere – Hannibalsaus](https://www.remiaprofessioneel.nl/producten/koude-sauzen/hannibalsaus/devlaendere-hannibalsaus-bokaal-3-l)).

**Colour/taste:** amber-brown; sweet, oniony, savoury, with **texture** from the onion
bits. A distinctive "insider" order.

---

## Zigeunersaus (gypsy sauce)

**What it is:** a **paprika-forward** sauce — base of red (bell) **paprika, onion, tomato
and gherkin**. Red paprika gives soft sweetness and colour. Famous with schnitzel, and
served with fries too ([VRT Dagelijkse Kost – Zigeunersaus](https://dagelijksekost.vrt.be/gerechten/zigeunersaus); [Culinaire Ambiance – Zigeunersaus](https://culinaireambiance.com/nl/articles/zigeunersaus-een-saus-met-paprika-in-de-schijnwerper)).

**Colour/taste:** red-orange; sweet-savoury pepper, mild-to-medium warmth, slightly
chunky.

**Name note:** the term *zigeuner* ("gypsy") is increasingly considered dated/offensive;
some brands have rebranded it (e.g. "paprika sauce"). Worth handling with a lighter touch
in a modern game — good candidate to rename in-world.

## Garlic, mustard & pickles

### Looksaus (garlic sauce)

**What it is:** a **garlic mayonnaise** — mayo heavy with garlic (Flemish *look* =
garlic). White, pungent, creamy. Ubiquitous, especially with kebab/mitraillette and
snacks; the "aïoli of the frituur."

### Mosterd (mustard)

**What it is:** table **mustard** — sharp, yellow-brown, vinegary. Less a fry dip than a
condiment for sausages (curryworst, cervela) and snacks, but always on the shelf. Belgium
has strong regional mustards (e.g. Tierenteyn of Ghent).

### Pickles / piccalilly (piccalilli)

**What it is:** a **chunky mustard-and-vinegar relish** of pickled vegetables —
gherkins, silver onions, cauliflower — bound in a **bright yellow, turmeric-mustard**
sauce, usually sweet-sour. Belgian *pickles* descend from English **piccalilli**, which
in turn took its mustard-turmeric idea from Indian pickling ([en.wikipedia – Piccalilli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piccalilli); [Frietopia – Piccalilly](https://www.frietopia.nl/frietopia/wiki/piccalilly/)).

**Colour/taste:** vivid yellow with visible veg; tangy, sweet-sour, mustardy, crunchy.
**Historically huge**: after WWII, virtually every *frietkot* served fries with
**mayonnaise or pickles** as the two default choices — pickles is one of the oldest
frituur sauces, predating most of the fancy modern ones.

## The hot gravy: stoofvleessaus / carbonade

**What it is:** not a cold sauce at all but a **hot beef-stew gravy** — the thick brown
sauce from **stoofvlees / stoverij / carbonade flamande**, Flemish beef-and-beer stew
(beef braised in Belgian brown ale with mustard, onions, bay, thyme, sometimes thickened
with mustard-spread bread) ([en.wikipedia – Flemish stew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flemish_stew)).

**Colour/taste:** deep brown; rich, savoury, malty-sweet from the beer, meaty. Ladled
hot over fries (with or without the meat) — comfort food, especially in cold months.

**Popularity — the Flemish trump card:** as **carbonnade / stoofvleessaus** it is a
**distant but clear #2** nationally (~21%). The regional split is dramatic: **27% of
Flemish** fry-eaters order it versus only **~4% of Walloons** — nearly **seven times**
more popular in the north. It's arguably Flanders' signature answer to Wallonia's
andalouse ([Brussels Times – "A saucy mystery"](https://www.brusselstimes.com/384182/a-saucy-mystery-how-do-belgians-like-their-fries); [Brussels Times – Stoofvlees: Belgium's favourite dish?](https://www.brusselstimes.com/361786/stoofvlees-belgiums-favourite-dish)).

## The classic combos & counter culture

- **Mayo-ketchup-ui** ("mayonnaise + ketchup + onions"): the iconic loaded order — a
  scoop of mayo, a stripe of ketchup, and a handful of **raw chopped onions** on top of
  the fries. Simple, cheap, beloved; the everyman's "works." Raw onion is the signature
  Belgian topping (also central to the *mitraillette* baguette and the Bicky burger).
- **Bicky saus:** the trio (yellow dressing + red tomato + brown "hot" sauce) tied to the
  **Bicky Burger**, a frituur cult item. The "hot" is more sweet-tangy than actually hot.
- **Sauce-list bragging:** the length of the sauce board is a point of pride and
  competition between frituren — some list **30+** sauces. Novelty/house sauces
  ("mystery," "tomagrec," seasonal specials) exist mainly to lengthen the board.
- **The "mammoet" gag:** ordering the giant *mammoet* frikandel *met mammoetsaus* is the
  frituur's built-in supersize joke.
- **Regional identity in a tub:** *what sauce you pick reads as where you're from* —
  andalouse ≈ Wallonia, stoofvleessaus ≈ Flanders, mayo everywhere.

## Brands & trademark notes

- **Devos & Lemmens (D&L):** ~130-year-old Belgian sauce house, 80+ varieties; its
  sweet **"Frites Sauce"** is engineered specifically for chips ([belgian-sauces.com – Frites Sauce](https://www.belgian-sauces.com/product/frites-sauce/)).
- **La William:** claims to have **invented sauce andalouse**; major frituur supplier
  (andalouse, brasil, riche, etc.) ([La William – About](https://lawilliam.be/en/about-la-william/)).
- **Vandemoortele:** big Belgian sauce/oils group; makes hannibal, mayo, specialties.
- **Anda Premium Sauces:** rival **andalouse** originator claim; brand literally named
  for *anda*louse.
- **Elite Salades & Snacks (NL):** owns the trademarked name **Joppiesaus**.
- **Bicky:** trademarked burger + dressing/hot/tomato sauce line.
- **Pauwels, Remia, DeVlaendere:** other common shelf brands across BE/NL.

Cold sauces should be **the highest-margin item in the shop** — that's the real economics
and it makes upgrading the sauce bar feel rewarding. Hot gravy is lower margin (labour +
ingredients) but a **loyalty/prestige** driver, especially in "Flanders" maps.

### Popularity weighting & regional demand

- Ship a **regional demand modifier** by map/neighbourhood:
  - **Wallonia map:** andalouse ×1.6, samurai ×1.3, ketchup ×1.2; stoofvleessaus ×0.4.
  - **Flanders map:** stoofvleessaus ×1.8, joppie ×1.4, mayo ×1.2; andalouse ×0.6.
  - **Brussels / mixed:** everything near baseline (great "sandbox" map).
- Weather / season: stoofvleessaus spikes in winter; cold sauces steady year-round.
- Customer archetypes: *Purist* (mayo only, judges you for ketchup), *Tourist* (ketchup,
  tips poorly, buys extra), *Teen crew* (samurai/joppie/mammoet, big combos),
  *Heat-seeker* (pili-pili, pays premium), *Traditionalist* (pickles + stoofvlees).

### Unlock progression (tech tree)

1. **Starter (1990s garage frietkot):** mayonnaise, ketchup, curry ketchup, pickles,
   mustard. The postwar defaults.
2. **Specialty tier I:** andalouse, cocktail, tartare, looksaus — unlock by reputation /
   first upgrade. This is the "now you're a real frituur" beat.
3. **Specialty tier II:** samurai, joppie (requires a **licensing deal** mini-event —
   nod to the trademark), brazil, zigeuner/paprika.
4. **Connoisseur tier:** riche, hannibal, américain, mammoet, pili-pili — long sauce
   board = prestige score.
5. **Hot line:** stoofvleessaus / carbonade needs a separate **stew station** building
   (slow-cook timer, beer + beef inputs) — a whole sub-economy.
6. **House sauces:** late-game **recipe designer** (see combos) lets the player invent and
   name a signature sauce.

Gate progression on **reputation + a "sauce board length" stat** — hitting 10, 20, 30
sauces unlocks cosmetic signage and passing-trade bonuses (mirrors real frituren bragging
about their board).

### "Sauce bar" upgrade line

A dedicated upgradeable fixture, escalating capacity, speed, freshness and flair:

- **Lv1 – Cooler tub row:** a few chilled tubs; limited to ~6 active sauces; manual
  scooping (slow, small spill/waste chance).
- **Lv2 – Pump dispensers:** faster serve, less waste, +active-sauce slots.
- **Lv3 – Refrigerated sauce wall / carousel:** 20+ slots, freshness meter slows,
  self-serve option (raises throughput, small theft/mess risk).
- **Lv4 – Automated dosing + "sauce gun":** near-instant serve, portion-perfect (margin
  up), enables **combo presets** at one tap.
- **Cross-upgrades:** *Freshness* (spoilage timer), *Portion control* (waste ↓, margin ↑),
  *Board display* (prettier menu → higher specialty attach rate), *Cold chain* (unlocks
  more simultaneous sauces).

Sauces have a **freshness/spoilage** stat: over-stocking rare sauces risks waste — a nice
inventory tension. Hot gravy has a **holding-time** limit (must be sold or dumped).

### Combo & special mechanics

- **Classic combos as one-tap presets** (bonus satisfaction + speed):
  - *"Mayo-ketchup-ui"* — mayo + ketchup + **raw chopped onions**; cheap, hugely popular,
    unlocks an **onion station**.
  - *"Bicky style"* — yellow + red + brown trio (pairs with a Bicky-burger menu item).
  - *"De grote"* — mammoet snack + mammoetsaus: the **supersize gag**, upsell prompt.
- **Combo attach-rate bonus:** offering the *right* sauce next to the *right* snack
  (curry ketchup + frikandel, looksaus + mitraillette, stoofvleessaus + fries) gives a
  satisfaction/tip multiplier — teaches the real pairings.
- **Heat ladder:** andalouse → samurai → pili-pili as an escalating "spice challenge"
  event; heat-seekers pay premiums, but too-spicy scares off *Purist* customers.
- **Regional-identity meta:** leaning your board toward andalouse vs stoofvleessaus shifts
  which customer archetypes you attract — a soft alignment choice with trade-offs.
- **House-sauce designer (late game):** pick base (mayo / tomato / gravy) + add-ins
  (peppers, chili, curry, pineapple, onion, pickle, garlic) → auto-generates colour, heat,
  cost, and a **popularity roll**. Name it and slap it on the board; a hit house sauce
  becomes a passive draw. Great achievement/humor hook (invent the next "andalouse").
- **Trademark event:** to sell "Joppiesaus" (or "Bicky") by name you must **license** it;
  otherwise sell a renamed clone at a small popularity penalty — a cheeky nod to the real
  trademark story, and a genuine cost/benefit choice.
- **The mustard-eyebrow / ketchup-shame flavour text:** *Purist* NPCs grumble when served
  ketchup on premium fries — pure comedy, and true to Belgian counter culture.
